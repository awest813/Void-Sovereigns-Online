# World Overview — Void Sovereigns Online

## Setting Summary

Void Sovereigns Online is set in a mid-future humanity-and-neighbors space opera universe. Technology is advanced but uneven — faster-than-light travel exists via the Void Relay network, but the network is aging, partially offline, and poorly understood. Daily life for most people is practical and difficult. The frontier is real and close.

The tone is NASA-punk: functional hardware, worn surfaces, patched ships, bureaucratic friction. The visual influence is late-90s anime and pulp science fiction — stylish, a little grubby, cool without being clean.

---

## The Void Relay Network

The Void Relay network is the backbone of interstellar civilization. Relay installations enable jump travel between star systems in hours rather than years. They were built during a period of rapid expansion several centuries ago using methods that are partially documented and partially lost.

**Current state:**
- Multiple Relays have gone offline in the last two decades
- Cause of failure is classified or unknown for many incidents
- New Relay construction has not occurred in living memory
- Offline Relay sites produce anomalous readings
- The Void Covenant studies them; nobody officially endorses this

The Void Relay network is the central mystery of the setting. Its decline is the reason the frontier is the way it is.

---

## Meridian Station

**The starter hub.**

Meridian Station is a massive converted mining platform that once served as a premier transfer hub for the region. Two nearby Void Relays went offline within three years of each other roughly twenty years ago. Traffic collapsed. The major shipping guilds rerouted. Corporate operations consolidated elsewhere.

What Meridian became: a low-rent but busy outpost for drifters, salvagers, haulers, freelancers, and people who cannot afford to go anywhere else. It is alive, barely. It smells like recycled air and lubricant.

- Factions: Meridian Dock Authority, Ashwake Extraction Guild, Free Transit Compact, Void Covenant (unofficial)
- Key NPCs: Dockmaster Renata Osei, Guild Rep Halvek Dross, Torrek Voss, Sova
- Services: contracts board, trade, docking, limited repairs

---

## Ashwake Belt

**The starter zone.**

The Ashwake Belt is an asteroid mining region a short jump from Meridian. Once productive, now mostly independent operators and abandoned corporate infrastructure. The rogue automation problem is escalating. Nobody has officially explained why.

- Tier 1–2 dungeon content
- Rogue mining equipment as primary enemies
- Secrets in the inactive survey grids

---

## Psionics

Psionics are rare, fringe, and socially distrusted. Psi sensitivity is associated with:
- Prolonged exposure to Void Relay sites and relics
- Unknown biological factors
- Rumored black-program experimentation (not confirmed in current canon)

Psi abilities are useful but come with social friction and systemic risks. The Void Covenant recruits psi-sensitive individuals. This should be presented as ambiguous — potentially protective, potentially predatory.

Psi is never flashy or dominant in the early game. It is strange and quiet.

---

## The Early Player Goal

The player begins on Meridian Station with a Cutter Mk. I — barely functional, not relay-capable.

The first major arc: earn enough to buy or rebuild a relay-capable ship and break out of Meridian's shrinking local economy.

This involves:
1. Taking contracts (salvage, bounty, delivery) in the Ashwake Belt
2. Accumulating credits and reputation
3. Upgrading the ship step by step
4. Eventually accessing a functional Void Relay

This is the core tension of Phase 1 content.

## Tone Notes

- The frontier is hard but not hopeless
- People are capable and practical, not helpless
- Cosmic horror is slow, background, and late
- No space elves; species are humanoid and practical
- Humor is dry, situational, and character-driven
- The universe does not care about the player; the people in it might
